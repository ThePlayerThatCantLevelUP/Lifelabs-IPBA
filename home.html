5 Whys

  1. Why were women's roles limited during the Tang Dynasty?

    Because societal norms and Confucian principles prescribed specific roles for men and women, emphasizing hierarchical relationships.

  2. Why did societal norms and Confucian principles prescribe specific roles for men and women?

    Because traditional Chinese cultural values, influenced by Confucianism, emphasized the importance of maintaining social order through adherence to prescribed roles.

  3. Why were traditional Chinese cultural values influenced by Confucianism?

    Because Confucianism, as a philosophical and ethical system, was deeply ingrained in the political and social fabric of the Tang Dynasty, shaping the expectations placed on individuals.

  4. Why was Confucianism deeply ingrained in the political and social fabric of the Tang Dynasty?

    Because Confucian principles were adopted as the official state ideology, influencing governance, education, and societal norms during this period.

  5. Why were Confucian principles adopted as the official state ideology during the Tang Dynasty?

    Because rulers and elites found Confucianism to be a stabilizing force that promoted social harmony and order, aligning with their vision for governance.
